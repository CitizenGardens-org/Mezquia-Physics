\begin{abstract}

This paper explores the mathematical formalization of the mind within the framework of the Information--Intent Nexus (IIN), operationalized in the IntentSim computational environment. Contrary to traditional views that treat mental phenomena as abstract or emergent from biology, the IIN posits intent as a fundamental, causal force inherently linked to information. IntentSim reveals the architecture of mental constructs in quantifiable form through simulations, presenting the Nexus Equations that model thoughts as structured Intent--Information Complexes (IICs). Simulation data suggest mental phenomena can be understood as structured fields governed by precise mathematical relationships, offering a novel pathway toward an empirically informed understanding of consciousness and cognition.

\end{abstract}
